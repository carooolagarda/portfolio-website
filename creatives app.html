00 The design process

01 The problem statement

The project brief included four application type, where I choose to develop the “Contact experts app”. Market research led me to focus on experts at creative fields like design, arts, photography, etc... due to the scarcity of such apps. As a designer, I've noticed design students' need to consult experienced professionals for unfamiliar projects.

To identify patterns, opportunities, and pain points of my future users, it was essential to conduct qualitative interviews with potential app users:

Problem statement

Potential users, such as designers and entrepreneurs, experience discomfort in their current process of contacting creative mentors for mentorship and/or collaboration

03 The user flow

A user flow was created to set how user will navigate the app & accomplish 3 important tasks: 1.Choose an expert, 2. Book a call & 3. Join and finish a booking call

04 The insight #1

Lack of prior experience can cause Jim & Anne to postpone or halt project initiation

Cause: Jim & Anne are insecure because they feel unprepared to execute the project

Need: Discover a proficient specialist in the needed field, alleviating any difficulties associated with reaching out to experts via social networks or personal contacts

Solution

An innovative app for booking mentorships featuring a wide-ranging selection of specialists across diverse creative fields, languages, and nations.

05 The insight #2

Monolingual users, like Jim, find it disruptive to their mentoring experience, if the chosen expert doesn’t speak their same language, possibly resulting in unbooking actions or project cancellation.

Cause: Anxiety over misunderstanding the expert, leads to questioning the value of their investment

Need: Possess the means to effectively communicate with experts in different languages or access a portfolio of experts fluent in their same language

Solution

The app features language and location filters for mentors and integrates a real-time translation tool for video calls

06 The insight #3

Anne & Jim, gain greater trust in mentors by reviewing feedback from other users and accessing session prices beforehand, fostering transparency and increasing the likelihood of completing the booking process

Cause: Uncertainty about affording the mentoring fee or resonating with the expert's guidance causes hesitation in contacting them

Need: Prior to engaging in direct communication with the mentor, the user requires access to this information

Solution

The profile page offers direct access to the mentor's rating, price range, and reviews from previous mentees. Additionally, after a video call, users can rate and provide feedback on the received service via a modal

Preference Testing

In Creatives app's Home screen, there are three sections: a search bar, a banner leading to the top 10 most creatives of the month, and suggested profiles based on user preferences.

In usability testing, 67% of participants failed to grasp the differentiation between these sections, prompting me to include clarifying elements: an arrow denoting the banner's interactivity and a widget title "SUGGESTED EXPERTS FOR YOU" positioned above the profile cards.

Among 11 participants, 82% preferred option #2, while 18% chose option #1, supported by comments emphasizing the clarity brought by "SUGGESTED FOR YOU" text and the arrow icon on the banner.

Using social media platforms like Instagram diverged from traditional testing methods, providing a familiar and accessible avenue to engage participants.
