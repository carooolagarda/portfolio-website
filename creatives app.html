Creatives

Easing the process of connecting with Creative Mentors worldwide, across diverse disciplines and multiple languages.

Overview

This project began as part of my UX/UI certification, where I was tasked with creating an app to connect users with experts. I focused on design mentors from diverse countries to provide a multilingual, globally accessible service, making creative expertise more inclusive and enriching for users worldwide.

Objective

Develop an application that applies UX and UI design techniques learned during my certification in a real-world context. The focus is on creating an app that is practical, functional, and tailored to the specific needs of its users.

The Design Process

As a design student, I noticed my classmates admired industry designers but often hesitated to seek their advice due to inexperience and fear. This inspired me to create an app that connects users with design experts, fostering a supportive mentorship environment.

To identify patterns, opportunities, and pain points of my future users, it was essential to conduct qualitative interviews with potential app users:

User flow

A user flow was designed to outline how users navigate the app to complete three key tasks: 1. Choose an expert, 2. Book a call, and 3. Complete the call.

Insight #1

Lack of prior experience can cause Jim and Anne to postpone or halt project initiation

Cause: Jim and Anne feel insecure and unprepared to execute the project.

Need: Find a proficient specialist in the required field, easing the challenges of reaching out to experts through social networks or personal contacts.

Solution: An innovative app for booking mentorships, offering a broad selection of specialists across diverse creative fields, languages, and countries.

Insight #2

Monolingual users, like Jim, find it disruptive to their mentoring experience if the chosen expert doesnâ€™t speak their language, possibly resulting in cancellations or unbooking actions.

Cause: Anxiety about misunderstanding the expert leads to questioning the value of the investment.

Need: Have a way to effectively communicate with experts in different languages or access a portfolio of experts fluent in their language.

Solution: Language and location filters for mentors and integrates a real-time translation tool for video calls.

Insight #3

Anne and Jim gain greater trust in mentors by reviewing feedback from other users and accessing session prices beforehand, fostering transparency and increasing the likelihood of completing the booking process.

Cause: Uncertainty about affording the mentoring fee or resonating with the expert's guidance causes hesitation in contacting them.

Need: Before engaging in direct communication with the mentor, the user requires access to this information.

Solution: Language and location filters for mentors and integrates a real-time translation tool for video calls.

User Interface Kit

The Creatives Design System features a modern aesthetic for users aged 18 to 40, fostering creativity and connecting them with expert guidance in a welcoming environment. Craft copy with a friendly, positive tone and prioritize empathy to enhance the user experience.

Test
Iterations

In the mid-fidelity test, users mistook the 'Top 10 banner' for a title, requiring visual separation. Arrow icons were added, and the banner image removed for clarity

Filters were consolidated onto one screen, improving navigation and efficiency, as confirmed by testing.

The initial design had a preview for swiping, but space constraints caused overcrowding. The final design prioritized a full-screen card, enhancing accessibility and improving hierarchy by placing the CTA button according to Fitts' Law.

The Creatives app's Home screen has three sections: a search bar, a banner for the top 10 creatives, and suggested profiles.

Usability testing showed 67% of users couldnâ€™t distinguish them, prompting the addition of an arrow for the banner and a 'SUGGESTED EXPERTS FOR YOU' widget.

Among 11 participants, 82% preferred option #2, while 18% chose option #1. Comments highlighted the clarity provided by the "SUGGESTED FOR YOU" text and the arrow icon on the banner.

Using social media platforms like Instagram deviated from traditional testing methods, offering a familiar and accessible way to engage participants.

Conclusion
Results

This project resulted in the UX and UI design of an app, tested with 8 users using various methods, with a 100% success rate in completing the 3 tasks in the user flow.

Challenges

As I was new to many of the topics during the certification, my learning curve was extended due to the extra time needed to grasp certain concepts. However, as I progressed and gained more experience in my work, I acquired the tools to deliver a reliable, tested, and user-friendly project.

Retrospective

I was intimidated by unfamiliar tools and design decisions, but I shifted my mindset by breaking down tasks, seeking feedback, and testing with users. This helped me build confidence and gain new skills. I plan to continue tackling challenges and improving my skills in future projects.
